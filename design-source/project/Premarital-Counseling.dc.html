What is premarital counseling?

The best investment in your marriage is the one you make before it starts.

Getting married is one of the most significant commitments you'll make. Premarital work sets the stage for understanding your partner more deeply — before the pace of life makes that harder to do. We use the Prepare & Enrich assessment as a structured starting point: naming what's already going well and surfacing the conversations worth having now, before they become harder to have later.

Frequently asked questions

Do we have to be engaged to start?+

No — you don't need a ring or a date to benefit from this work. Some couples come in while seriously dating and considering engagement. The Prepare & Enrich assessment tailors its questions to where you are in your relationship, so it works for both dating and engaged couples. If you're in a committed relationship and thinking about a future together, this is a worthwhile investment at any stage.

What is the Prepare & Enrich assessment?+

Prepare & Enrich is a research-backed relationship assessment developed by Dr. David Olson. It maps your strengths and growth areas across 12 relationship domains — communication, conflict, finances, family background, intimacy, spirituality, and more. Each partner completes it independently online, and the results become a detailed report we review together. Studies show it reduces the risk of divorce by 31%.

Do we have to be religious to work with you?+

Not at all. The assessment includes questions about spiritual beliefs because they're relevant to shared values and meaning-making, but you don't need a faith background to benefit. I welcome couples from all backgrounds — and if faith is meaningful in your relationship, I'm very comfortable working within that context.

How many sessions does it take?+

I offer two packages. The Marriage Prep Package includes 8 sessions — enough to review the assessment and focus on the areas most relevant to you. The Complete Premarital Program includes 12 sessions, suited for couples who want to examine the domains more thoroughly. We'll talk through which fits during your free consultation.

How is this different from a premarital course?+

Courses and workshops deliver a curriculum — useful, but broadly designed. What I offer is personalized, attachment-informed time grounded in the specific results of your Prepare & Enrich assessment and your particular histories. Rather than a predetermined agenda, we follow what's most relevant for you as a couple.

Can we continue as couples therapy clients afterward?+

Absolutely — and many couples do. Premarital counseling is a great foundation, and some couples want to continue the work as they move into the first year of marriage and the changes that come with it.
