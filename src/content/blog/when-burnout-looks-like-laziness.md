---
title: "When burnout looks like laziness"
excerpt: "For creatives whose worth got tangled up in output — a slower way back to the work."
category: Creativity
date: 2026-06-03
---

For a lot of creative people, self-worth and output get quietly fused early on — you are what you make, and how much of it. So when burnout hits and the work stops coming, it rarely gets read as burnout. It gets read as laziness, as losing your edge, as something being wrong with you rather than with the pace you've been keeping.

Recovering from creative burnout usually isn't about finding the right productivity system. It's about loosening that fusion — building a relationship with your work, and with yourself, that doesn't collapse the moment output slows down.
